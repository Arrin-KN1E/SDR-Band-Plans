Satellite: GOES-16 

Satellite details
Acronym GOES-16
Full name Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellite - 16
Satellite Description
  • 1st flight unit of the GOES 3rd generation programme.
  • Mission: operational meteorology.
  • Substantial contribution to space weather.
Mass at launch 5500 kg Dry mass 2800 kg
Power 4000 W
Data access link http://cimss.ssec.wisc.edu/goes/goesdata.html
Data access information
  • Near-real time availability of ABI, GLM, EXIS, MAG, SUVI and SEISS (MPS, EHIS and SGPS) by GRB and LRIT.
  • Near real-time data available at GEONETCAST Americas, see http://www.geonetcastamericas.noaa.gov/.
  • Data and products available from the PDA (restricted).
  • Real-time availability of EMWIN-N information.
  • DCP messages through ground lines from the CDA.
  • Alert messages of GEOS&R to LUT in real time.
  • Overview over Data Access Methods at

http://www.goes-r.gov/users/user-systems.html

Orbit Geostationary orbit Altitude 35786 km
Longitude 75.2° W

 

Space agency NOAA , NASA
Status Operational
Details on Status
(as available)
  • After a long-lasting commissioning phase at 89.5°W, the satellite has been moved to 75.2°W, reached in mid-December 2017.  It has been declared operational on 18 December 2017.
  • Primary GOES-East satellite.
Launch 19 Nov 2016 EOL ≥2027
Last update: 2021-05-20
